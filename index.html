Exploration Expenditure

Australia has witnessed a steady increase in mineral exploration expenditure for all deposits, totalling to about $1 billion this year. Consequently, the exploration expenditure for gold, iron ore, coal, base metals and others has also grown steadily over the past 7 years. In contrast, expenditure for coal has remained more or less the same over the years. Expenditure for gold was at an all time high between 2021 and 2023.

Average Mineral Production by State

The country witnessed a drastic shift in the mineral market when it comes to statewise production. From 1860 to 1920, New South Wales accounted for about 80% of Australia's mineral production. However since then, for the last century, Victoria and Western Australia have been dominating the mineral market.

1851 - 1870 witnessed the Gold Rush in Australia. During this time, Australia accounted for one third of the world's gold production. This pivotal period is reflected in the chart.

Brush over the time axis of the mineral production chart to visualise how the distribution of production varies across all states.

Exploration Expenditure on Different Deposits

Exploration expenditure for existing and new deposits refers to the financial investment made to discover and assess mineral resources. There has been consistent expenditure on exploration over the last 7 years. A total of around $1B has been spent on exploring all total deposits in 2024. Out of this, about $712M accounted for exploring existing sites while about $303M was spent on exploring new ones.

This analysis is critical to strike a balance between enhancing capability and production potential of existing deposits while also innovating an exploring for future needs.
